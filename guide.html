Marking Console

Guide · v1.0.0 Open the app →

A tracker for working through a large marking load: per paper, per section, across classes and devices.

The tool offloads two things only: memory (which mistakes have I already seen) and drafting (the wording of feedback). Every judgement stays with you.

Quick start

  1. Set up (top right) → add a class: give it a name and paste your roster, one student per line.
  2. On the dashboard, + New marking job → pick the class, name the assessment, set the due date.
  3. Resume the job and mark. Tick each paper; the dashboard keeps score and paces your days.

Recipes

Each is a situation, then exactly what to do.

Several jobs, different deadlines

11PSC's test is due Sunday; 13PHY's on Tuesday. Which do I push today?

In the app

Give each job its own due date when you create it. The dashboard's Today's quota splits the day across jobs by urgency, so the Sunday job shows the bigger share to do today. Work down that list from the top.

An assessment with sections

The test has Section A, B and C, and I want to hold the same standard across all of Section A before I move on.

In the app

When creating (or editing) the job, put each section on its own line in Parts (leave blank for a single-part assessment). While marking, pick a part tab and go down the whole class; finishing a part jumps you to the next. Leave Parts blank and it behaves as one whole-paper mark.

The same mistake, again and again

Half the class has dropped significant figures.

In the app

Type it once in the Trip hazards column (right), then click it on each paper where you see it. It counts as you go. Copy hazard summary gives you the frequency table for planning your reteach.

A feedback comment for a student

I want a short, honest comment for Aroha.

In the app

On her paper, Copy feedback prompt, then paste into Claude. The prompt uses only the trip hazards and notes you logged (grouped by section), and asks for roughly 80 words, encouraging but honest, inventing nothing.

A student was away

Sam was absent today, I must chase it — versus Sam sat out the whole unit, which is settled.

In the app

On the paper, choose Absent — follow up (shown in amber, counted on the dashboard so you do not forget) or Not sitting (quiet, kept off the dashboard). When a followed-up student sits later: Mark present, mark the work, and set Sat on to the day they sat.

Flagging for moderation

A borderline script I want a second opinion on.

In the app

On the paper, Flag for moderation and add a note. Flagged papers show a ⚑ in the roster and appear, with your note, in Copy assessment data.

Fixing a tick

I ticked the wrong student, or a mark changed after moderation.

In the app

Click the status box next to a name in the roster to tick or untick it, or use Un-mark on the open paper. No score is ever locked.

Marking on two devices

I start on the laptop and finish at a cafe on my phone.

In the app

Set up → Sync across devices. Make a GitHub token (classic, scope gist), paste it, Sync now. On the phone, paste the same token and the gist id. It merges both ways, so marking Section A on one device and Section B on the other both survive.

Sharing results

I want a record, or something to analyse.

In the app

Copy hazard summary for the reteaching table, or Copy assessment data for a full anonymous dump of one assessment (papers numbered, no names) that you can file or paste into an AI to ask "how did this go".

Privacy

Student names live only in your browser, and, if you turn on sync, in a secret (unlisted) GitHub gist. The app file and the public site contain no student data, which is why they are safe to host and to share.
